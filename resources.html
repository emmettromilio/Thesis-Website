Find your starting point

Every great project begins the same way — with a thought that hasn’t taken shape yet. The ideation phase is the most personal part of any creative process, and also the most misunderstood. Invulb exists to make this moment feel less daunting and more like play.

Understanding the ideation phase

Ideation is the stage before the work begins — when you’re gathering instincts, making connections, and figuring out what you actually want to say. It’s not about having the right answer. It’s about giving yourself permission to explore without judgment.

The best ideas rarely arrive fully formed. They show up in fragments, in doodles, in random words jotted at 2am. Invulb is built to hold those fragments safely while you figure out what they mean.

For Designers

Start with feeling, not function. Before you open any design software, ask yourself: what emotion should this project create? Build a moodboard around that feeling first.

Try this: use the mindmap method to gather ideas before you touch a single shape or frame.

For Writers

Writers often get stuck because they’re trying to write and edit at the same time. Separate those two processes. In the ideation stage, your only job is to get it out — not to get it right.

Try this: use Synapse's note pad to write without stopping for five minutes. Don’t delete anything. See what shows up.

For Artists

Visual artists often think better through their hands than their heads. If you’re stuck, don’t plan — sketch. Fill a scratch page with thumbnails, quick gestures, and visual questions.

Try this: Use the lotus diagram and pick one word that captures your concept and branch outward freely.
