Explore Ideation Methods

Each method is a structured approach to generating and exploring ideas. Learn how each one works, when to use it, and how to get the most out of your ideation session.

MIND MAP

Start with your core idea

Mind mapping is a visual brainstorming technique that helps you explore ideas and their relationships. Start with a central concept and branch out to related ideas.

1

Write your central idea in the middle

2

Branch out with related sub-ideas

3

Connect related ideas with lines

FISHBONE

Identify cause and effect

The Fishbone diagram helps you explore causes and effects. Perfect for analyzing problems or understanding complex relationships between ideas.

1

Define your main issue or goal

2

Identify major categories of causes

3

Add specific factors under each category

SAILBOAT

Navigate goals and obstacles

The Sailboat method helps teams reflect on goals, obstacles, and resources. It's a great retrospective tool for understanding what helps or hinders progress.

1

Define your destination or goal

2

Identify wind (helping forces)

3

Identify anchors (obstacles)

SCAMPER

Modify and improve

SCAMPER is a checklist method that prompts you to think about modifications to improve your idea. Each letter represents a different thinking strategy.

1

Go through each SCAMPER prompt

2

Note ideas that spark from each prompt

3

Combine and refine your best ideas

LOTUS DIAGRAM

Expand ideas systematically

The Lotus Diagram helps you break down a central idea into manageable sub-ideas. It's perfect for planning complex projects or organizing thoughts.

1

Place your central idea in the center

2

Create 8 surrounding boxes for sub-ideas

3

Expand each sub-idea further
